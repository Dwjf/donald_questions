Introduction

This page is dedicated to the questions raised by Donald, who asked me some questions about Christianity and the Bible.

1. During Noah's time the flood destroyed the world because at that time the sin was full. Why didn't Jesus die for man's sin during Noah (much earlier) than during His appearance during the Roman Empire?

First of all I'm impressed that Donald knew some parts of the bible and then came up with this line of questioning which basically critiqued the purpose of Jesus appearing and God's timing of it

I believe, to understand Jesus's sacrifise for sins of mankind 2025 years ago, we would need to see the unfolding of history, and the various ways God deals with sin since the beginning of time, and the birth of Jesus Christ and his sacrifise and the final solution to sin. You need to look at God's plan throughout history , according to the bible, before the final solution , which is the sacrifise of Christ.

God's plan thruout creation can be summarised in the bible, which consists of 66 books, divided into the Old testament and the New Testament, which records about 6000 years of history. Below is a table showing a summary of the books of the bible and when it was written

Table 1

Summary of important timeline in the Bible

In a short summary, the bible records important timelines below:

  1. Creation of earth (Genesis)
  2. Life before the creation of Jewish people. Adam, Cain and Able, Noah (Genesis)
  3. Life of the ancestors of the Jewish people. Abraham, Isaac, Jacob and his 12 sons (Genesis)
  4. The beginnings of the Jewish people (Genesis)
  5. How they ended up in Egypt and how they were led out of Egypt by Moses (Exodus)
  6. How God, thru Moses establish the beginning of the Jewish kingdom, establishing Laws for the country, Establishing the worship of God and his temple, establishing the Army, and the conquest of the new land which they made their own country. (Leviticus,Numbers, Deuteronomy, Joshua)
  7. The conversion from a country led by Judges to a single King (Judges, 1 Samuel, 2 Samuel, 1 Kings, 2 Kings)
  8. The destruction of the Jewish Kingdom by the major kingdoms in the Middle east during that time: The Assyrians, Bablylonians, Persians and Roman Empire (1 Chronicles, 2 Chronicles)
  9. The lives of Jewish people during their time of slavery under the major kingdoms (Ezra, Nehemiah, Esther,Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, Daniel, Hosea, Joel, Amos, Obadiah, Micah, Nahum, Habakkuk,Zephaniah, Haggai, Zechariah, Malachi)
  10. The birth of Jesus during the Roman Empire (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John)
  11. And his teachings, crucification, and Resurrection
  12. The writings of his disciples about the life and teachings of Jesus
  13. How the church developed during the 1st Century AD (Acts)
  14. Teachings about Christ and writings/letters by apostles, disciples to churches (Romans, 1 Corithians, 2 Corinthians, Galations, Ephesians, Phillippians, Colossians, 1 Thessalonians, 2 Thessalonians, 1 Timothy, 2 Timothy)
